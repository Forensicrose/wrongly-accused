Stories of the Wrongly Convicted

Deoxyribonucleic Acid, known widely as DNA, provides the instructions to every living thing on earth. It is also routinely used in criminal investigations and to assist with legal proceedings. As it pertains to exonerations, DNA is at the forefront. The first DNA exoneration occurred in 1989 and since then there have been approximately 375 exonerees due to DNA analysis. There are numerous stories about individuals accused of crimes they did not commit, serving lengthy prison sentences and sitting on death row. The accusations and prosecutions are swift and typically rely on eye witness identification, police coercion and jailhouse informants.
